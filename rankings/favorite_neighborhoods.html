Top 5 Favorite Neighborhoods

5) Cuba St - Wellington, New Zealand

The kind of place where you need to know where the bar is in advance to find it, a bit too cool for school so they don’t need signs here. The young cultural hotspot for New Zealand’s capitol, cafes, bars and bookshops line the pedestrian-only streets. One of the few safe spots to walk in a country where car is king.

4) Nihonbashi - Tokyo, Japan

Festive neighborhood with all the cool little spots (jazz bars, basement sushi, izakayas filled with salarymen) that make Tokyo special. Lacks the bright lights of Shinjuku or Shibuya, and some of the tourist crowds too.

3) Chinatown - Singapore

The sole of Singapore’s traditional neighborhoods that still feels lived in by locals. Hawker centers with culinary delights, beautiful temples and murals, and a bar scene where you might actually find someone who is Singaporean.

2) Old Quarter - Hanoi, Vietnam

The chaotic lifeblood of Vietnam is on full display. Beautiful Hoan Kiem lake and delicious food throughout.

1) Enmore / Newtown - Sydney, Australia

Probably the neighborhood that’d I could most see myself living in. Lots of bookstores and a good indie movie theater. Great mix of spots to eat (lively middle eastern and east asians restaurants abound) and drink (breweries, dives, wine bars, even a gimmicky self-serve beer bar).
