Favorite Hikes

Honorable Mentions

Giant Stairway - Blue Mountains, Australia

A sheer ascent and descent so rapid my ears popped. The blue mountains are incredibly cool, think the topography of Utah with mesas and cliffs but instead of a desert there is a huge forest draped on top. Climbing down on a thousand stairs from the cliff top to an ever deepening forest is a perfect way to truly appreciate the unique landscape. If asked what was my favorite hike that’s under an hour this would probably be it, but probably a little too short to qualify for my top 5 overall.

Rob Roy Glacier - Mt Aspiring National Park, New Zealand

A hike that’s really all about the destination. Walking up, seeing little peeks through the trees of the glacier sitting on the ridge line were exhilarating. Once the full view of the glacier was revealed, expanding beyond a full field of vision with its 20+ waterfalls, it was like praying at an altar of nature.

Mount Miyanoura - Yakushima, Japan

This is an odd one to include because I didn’t do the whole trail nor made it to the peak. It was the one trail on this list I hiked in the winter, so snow and daylight hours was an issue. But the experience feels worth including as I spent most of the hike in the clouds that were covering the forested hills. Yakushima was a major inspiration for Mayazaki’s films and walking through those woods of mossy stumps and twisted branches with mists blowing amongst it all I could feel why

Kepler Track - Fiordland, New Zealand

Maybe my favorite stretch of hiking ever is on this trail, riding a ridgeline for five miles with panoramic views of rolling mountains in every direction. I walked pretty slow with frequent breaks to soak it all in. That stretch is why you complete the nearly 37 mile trail, but on either side of it is a lot of flat woods walking. It’s a beautiful forest, lush and green where ferns and mosses abound, but 20+ miles of it can be a lot.


Top 5 Favorite Hikes (*from my trip, so non-US division)

5) Tongariro Alpine Crossing - Tongariro National Park, New Zealand

Hikes are usually through landscapes carved by water and ice, rivers and glaciers, so a landscape molded by lava is something unique. That’s why the Alpine Crossing deserves a shout, an otherworldly desert sitting on a ridge between two volcanoes. Perfect little blue pools sit there with wisps of sulfuric steam rising out of the soil around them. And one of those volcanoes is the stand in for Mt Doom, a beautiful mountain perfectly shaped as a cone. On its front side you can map out where different lava flows have streamed down over the years.

4) Clifftop Track / Grand Canyon Loop - Blue Mountains, Australia

Everything that the Blue Mountains have to offer in a single hike. Gorgeous views overlooking epic valleys and cliffs. Plants clinging to the ridgelines of the Blue Mountains like they are the hanging gardens of Babylon. And then you descend into Australia’s grand canyon, quite the opposite of America’s: a narrow little canyon with lush greenery, it feels primordial, like you are entering a hidden world.

3) Main Range Track - Snowy Mountains, Australia

The hike encompasses the highest ridgeline of Australia, including the high peak of the continent: Mount Kosciuszko. There’s a short way up involving a ski lift, but the long way is worth it. The trail offers excellent bang for your buck, the road takes you basically to the edge of the treeline so the whole hike offers uninterrupted views from the highest points in Australia. You can see the country slope off in either direction below you from the top of the ridge.

2) Routeburn Track - Fiordland National Park / Mt Aspiring National Park, New Zealand

Routeburn track offers a little bit of everything: mossy woods, expansive views from ridgelines, mountain passes with peaks rising around, narrow walks shadowed by cliffs. The hike truly feels like going on a journey, the two trailheads of four hours drive apart via car. The trail cleaves through the mountain range while the roads must go around.

1) Muller’s Hut - Aoraki / Mt Cook National Park, New Zealand

This was my favorite hike because the scenery of Mt Cook National Park was my favorite of any I visited. The landscape is a beautiful tapestry of colors, green and grey ridge walls, white and black mountain peaks, with flat plains running below. The hike is steep, but the view of Mt Cook is breathtaking from up high.
