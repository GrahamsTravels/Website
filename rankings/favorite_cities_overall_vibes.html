Honorable Mentions

Can Tho, Vietnam

Largest city in the Mekong delta, big rivers intersect here and the waterfronts are very nice, and lively with street life and festivals. I was lucky enough to see a street music fest that varied from teen rock bands to old men playing some traditional instruments (at least trying to above the general din of the festival). Although, during the day, the large city (over 1 million people) seems downright sleepy compared to Hanoi and Saigon.

Seoul, South Korea

Korea’s largest metropolis and capital is a diverse blend of neighborhoods. From marketplace-laden alleyways to the shopping malls of Gangnam, from hip streets with cafes and delicious BBQ restaurants to the monument to horizontal corporate integration that is Lotteeworld.

Sydney, Australia

The beaches are the stars of the show and stellar, and there are some cool neighborhoods farther from the coast. Downtown smells a bit too touristy and corporate, but there are still some hidden gems in there, and the opera house / harbor bridge are famous for a reason.


Top 5 Favorite Cities

5) Wellington, New Zealand

A beautiful city with a fun vibe that is juxtaposed with the more sterile (but also beautiful) Auckland. Lively cafe and bar culture surrounded by glorious mountains.

4) Singapore

An incredibly interesting city for all its accusations as fascist disneyworld. Good museums, food and green spaces make it a great place to travel to, not sure about residing there though.

3) Nagasaki, Japan

Interesting and tragic history across a few different museums. Great food from the natural fusions of Japanese and Chinese (Champon, basically Chinese ramen), and Japanese and Western (turoku rice that combines tonkatsu, rice pilaf and spaghetti). Plus, incredible sashimi. The city is also beautifully nestled in a mountainous inlet and has a plethora of fun narrow streets and alleys to get lost in.

2) Tokyo, Japan

The largest city in the world, a massive sprawl with three different downtowns. Varied neighborhoods feel like cities to themselves, there is something for everyone here. And despite its massive scale, it's a city of intimate cozy corners hidden throughout. Tokyo is filled to the brim with tiny little restaurants, bars, cafes and stores to stumble upon.

1) Hanoi, Vietnam

I just love the chaotic streets of Hanoi. The city is so filled with life that anywhere else feels quiet and quaint by comparison. The whizzing traffic and congested sidewalks mean this isn’t a city you can zone out in. Nor would you want to, every block has something interesting going on, little businesses or just exciting social life. There is something beautiful about the eclectic building design that resembles miss-sized boxes with houseplants draping over them (as elsewhere in Vietnam, Hanoi is green, can’t stop plant life in the tropics).
