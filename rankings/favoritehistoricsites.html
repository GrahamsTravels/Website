Top 5 Historic Sites

Fushimi Inari Shrine - Kyoto, Japan

The famous red gates bespeckle the whole mountain. There’s a lengthy climb up through the gates, but there are lots of pretty little spots just off the main track with shrines nestled within the bamboo forests. Little fox statues with their red bibs guide the way.

4) Nijo Castle - Kyoto, Japan

The best part of the shogun’s old palace is probably inside, which is decorated with panel art that showcases Japan’s deep love of nature. Plus, the floors squeak like bird calls so guards would know if there are intruders which is neat. Taking photos is banned inside (probably for the best, social media photographers infest Kyoto), so I only have pictures of the outside, but there’s some beautiful carving work done there too.

3) Temple of Literature - Hanoi, Vietnam

The centuries’ old centerpiece of education in Vietnam, the school for generations of scholars who would go on to run the nation. At least before the French shrewdly turned it into a trumpeters’ school. It's a very beautiful collection of gardens and buildings, and the plaques are very informative by Vietnamese museum standards. Bonus points because a high school graduation was going on when we went there.

2) Great Buddha Temple - Nara, Japan

Known for the giant statue of Buddha that stands (or rather sits) over 40 feet tall. The walk up to the temple that houses the Buddha is perhaps even better with layers of entrances and courtyards. The best spot is probably a gate guarded by a pair of fearsome demons.

Himeji Castle - Himeji, Japan

A remarkably well-preserved castle, both inside and out. Japanese castles are known for their trickery. The secret to their defenses is how they lead invaders the wrong way and trap them. That’s very obvious to see in Himeji. The grounds are extensive, with winding walls and paths, dead-ends and hidden gates.
