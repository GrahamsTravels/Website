Favorite Cities Part 1:
Most Beautfiul Cities

So, the most beautfiul cities can mean a couple of different things, it can mean the city itself, the buildings, how the city is laid out, etc. Oh it can mean a city in a very beatiful place. I figured this is worth two lists, scenic, as in the most beutiful location will come first, then the most beatiful for the cities themselves.

Top 5 Most Scenic Cities

5) Nagasaki, Japan

Hills loom above all around Nagasaki which sits beside an inlet. The day I was there the mountains played hide and seek with the clouds, adding to the beauty. During Japan’s isolation period, Dutch traders were confined to a single man-made island in Nagasaki harbor. What must they have thought of this strange foreign land when all they could see were these mysterious cloud-topped mountains.

4) Busan, Korea

Beautiful craggy beaches sit at the bottom of weaving hills in Busan. Colorful houses enliven those hillsides as they sprawl downs towards the water. And a great blue sky rises above all.

3) Sydney, Australia

The city of Sydney itself isn’t much to look at until you get to the harbor, which is why it’s on this list and not the following one. But the harbor is beautiful, including the man-made additions with the bridge and the famous opera house. And the beaches are spectacular, that’s why Sydney deserves to be on this list. The walk from Coogee to Bondi is diverse in its beauty, and Manly is one of the most beautiful beaches in the world. City beaches that rival any remote tropical island.

2) Wellington, New Zealand

The city of Wellington itself is hemmed in on all sides by mountains and water. Sitting at the harbor you can see great walls of mountain rising up on either side. And as with any mountain, the views from the top are stunning.

1) Auckland, New Zealand

Like Sydney, Auckland isn’t much to look at away from the harbor, but it is a harbor that stands unrivaled from all other harbors for beauty. The water is a beautiful shade of light blue, and Mt Rangitoto (a baby by volcano standards at only 500 years old) overlooks everything

Top 5 Most Beautfiul Cities

5) Hoi An, Vietnam

An old trading hub that has been kept well-preserved. Sitting along a river, the old buildings are beautiful with pockets of plantlife growing expansively. It’s at night when the city is most stunning as the river lights up with floating candles and reflections of buildings.

4) Dunedin, New Zealand

Old buildings and churches are everywhere here, and it probably helps that it could have made the scenic top 5 too… like everywhere else in New Zealand, the landscapes are spectacular. ‘Dunedin is the Scottish translation for Edinburgh as Scottish immigrants tried to rebuild their capital in a new land, and between the beautiful churches and ubiqiqous hills they largely succeeded.

3) Melbourne, Australia

Similar to Dunedin with beautiful old buildings everywhere. Pretty remarkable that such a “new” city has such a rich architectural display.

2) Singapore

Singapore has the perfect mix of old and new. Colorful and narrow homes line many city streets. The city is dotted by beautiful temples and mosques, including the colorful steeples of Hindu temples. And the new skyscrapers are designed with an interest in pleasing the eye, often working plants into their facades.

1) Kyoto, Japan

It’s a city with 17 UNESCO heritage sites, so it would be hard to pick anything else here. Temples galore, each with something unique. There are whole neighborhoods of Kyoto that feel like stepping back in time.

Bonus Ugliest City:

Canberra, Australia

What the fuck is this shit? It’s a planned capital city but apparently the plan was to make it look like a giant office park that sits next to a highway. The capital building itself is nice, but you literally have to walk by underpasses to get there. There are a few pretty angles with the lakes in the foreground and mountains behind, but that just proves they took a scenic area and dumped an ugly office park on top of it. And the whole thing is super spread out too, you basically need a car to get anywhere here.
