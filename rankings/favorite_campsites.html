Top 5 Favorite Campsites

5) Noah’s Beach - Daintree Rain Forest, Australia

Partially biased because this was the first campsite of my long multi-country road trip. Even if the crocodile and jellyfish-infested waters prevented swimming, there’s something special about sitting on a beach seeing rain forest covered mountains slope into the sea. And even more special is falling asleep in a rain forest that you can hear teeming with life in the night.

4) Upper Eglinton Valley - Fiordland National Park, New Zealand

Beautiful campsite that lacks the crowds of vans that plague many NZ campsites. And perfectly located for exploring Fiordland.

3) Kylie’s Beach - Crowdy Neck National Park, Australia

This is basically because it had the best kangaroo sightings from my time in Australia. And I saw a great view of the milky way at night. Nice bluffs and beach to walk along too.

2) Youngoona Campground - Flinders Ranges National Park, Australia

Super remote campsite where I spent New Year’s Eve. I could hear red kangaroos hopping around in the dark all night as I watched the stars. And at dusk I had a great view of the razorback, the Flinders Ranges’ most distinctive geographic feature.

1) A few hundred yards off the main range trail - Snowy Mountains, Australia

A backpacking site recommended by a ranger. Spent the night all alone on the roof of Australia. There’s something about watching a sunset at the top of the world.
